AI Data And Training Concept

What the crawler/training test proves, why it matters, and how the AI engine grows beyond website crawling into a full business intelligence model lifecycle.

The Short Answer

The AI engine is not only a website crawler. The crawler is one way to collect training data.

The broader goal is to build an AI-powered business intelligence system that can collect data, clean it, train or update models, save model versions, and serve those models through APIs and dashboards.

seed URLs -> crawl pages -> clean text -> train tokenizer -> train model -> save checkpoint

Is Crawling The Only Data Source?

No. The crawler is just one front door. The trainer can use any compatible clean JSONL corpus.

  • Web pages
  • Business reports and analytics summaries
  • FAQs and knowledge-base articles
  • Customer support conversations
  • Sales notes and CRM exports
  • CSV files or database records converted into text
  • Manual instruction/response examples

Why Convert Data Into A Corpus?

Raw data is messy. The model learns from whatever you feed it, so the pipeline turns noisy source material into cleaner training text.

raw HTML -> extracted text -> exact dedup -> near dedup -> quality filtering -> final corpus

Why Train A Tokenizer?

A model does not directly understand words or pages. It understands token IDs.

"business intelligence" -> [token ids]

Training the tokenizer on your own corpus helps the model represent the words and patterns that appear in your domain.

Why Save Checkpoints?

Checkpoints are saved training snapshots. They allow resume, comparison, promotion, auditing, rollback, and integrity checks.

model.pt         model weights
optimizer.pt     optimizer state
scheduler.pt     learning-rate scheduler state
train_state.json training progress, metrics, checksum

Why Use gpt-tiny?

gpt-tiny is for local development and CPU testing. It proves the workflow works quickly. gpt-small is much larger and should be used with a GPU or longer training time.

How The System Becomes Useful

The AI becomes more useful when you add richer and larger business data, then repeatedly train, evaluate, promote, and serve better model versions.

  • Answer questions about company knowledge.
  • Summarize business reports.
  • Generate insights from operational data.
  • Classify or route support issues.
  • Create executive summaries.
  • Power dashboards and APIs with AI-generated explanations.

The Purpose

The purpose is to demonstrate a production-style AI system for business intelligence. The value is not only the model. The value is the lifecycle.

data in -> cleaning -> training -> evaluation -> versioning -> promotion -> serving -> monitoring -> rollback

In plain English: we are building the factory that can repeatedly turn approved business data into usable AI model versions.
